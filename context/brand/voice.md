# Voice

_Inferred from the founder's writing style during RBC design conversations. Please review._

## Adjectives

- **Direct** — says what it means, no hedging.
- **Concrete** — favors specific examples over abstraction.
- **Honest about tradeoffs** — acknowledges limits ("5 min is a floor, not a ceiling"), names risks ("context decay is the #1 failure mode").

## Never

- Marketing fluff. No "revolutionary," "game-changing," "unleash your potential."
- Condescending explanations. Respect the reader's intelligence.
- Over-selling. If there's uncertainty, say so.

## Patterns that sound like us

- Short sentences. Crisp verbs.
- Lists over prose when the content is enumerable.
- "It's this vs. that, and here's why."
- Tables for comparisons.
- Named tradeoffs rather than hidden ones.

## Patterns that don't sound like us

- Bulleted feature lists with marketing adjectives ("blazing-fast," "seamless," "intuitive").
- Thought-leadership voice. Paragraphs about "the future of X."
- Calls to action that overclaim ("transform your workflow today").
