# Personas

## Primary — Solopreneur-Developer (ICP)

- **Role:** Founder and developer of their own product, usually SaaS, web apps, or AI tools.
- **Context:** Works solo or with 1–2 collaborators. Ships often (multiple deploys per day is normal).
- **Tooling:** Claude Code as primary agent; IDE is Cursor, VS Code, or similar. Heavy AI usage — AI writes a meaningful share of code, copy, and decisions.
- **Frustrations:** Re-explaining their ICP, pricing, or brand voice to AI on every session. Agents making feature suggestions that ignore business constraints. Documentation tools (Notion, Confluence) create context fragmentation.
- **Wins from RBC:** Agents stop guessing. Business intent survives long gaps between sessions. Five minutes of onboarding saves hours across the year.

## Secondary — Very Small Team (1–3)

- Small founding team working primarily in one repo.
- Some role overlap (one person writes code, copy, and does ops).
- Benefits most from the living-document property — the team's shared mental model converges on `/context/`.

## Not a fit

- Large organizations with dedicated product/marketing/ops teams and mature docs in Jira/Confluence.
- Multi-repo setups with services owned by different teams.
- Throwaway prototypes or one-off scripts.
- Teams or people that prefer to document the business context outside the repository (e.g. Confluence, Waybook, etc.)
- Teams or people that prefer to document/organize tasks outside the repository (e.g. Jira, Asana, etc.)
